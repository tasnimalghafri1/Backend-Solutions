The Agile Manifesto is a document created in 2001 by a group of software
developers who wanted to find a better way to develop software. It defines the core
values and principles of Agile development, which emphasize flexibility,
collaboration, and customer satisfaction.
The Four Core Values of the Agile Manifesto:
1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation.
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation.
4. Responding to change over following a plan.
How it differs from traditional project management:
 ----------------------------------- Problems they were trying to solve in the software industry:
1. Projects took too long and often delivered software that no longer met
customer needs.
2. Changing requirements were hard to handle with rigid plans.
3. There was poor communication between developers, managers, and clients.
4. Teams often delivered software full of bugs or that didn’t provide real value
